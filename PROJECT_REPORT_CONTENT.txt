CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence, particularly in the domain of Large Language Models (LLMs), has opened unprecedented opportunities for developing intelligent systems that can understand and process human language with remarkable accuracy. This project presents the design and implementation of a Diet LLM Recommendation System—an intelligent, AI-powered platform that enables users to log their dietary intake using natural language and receive personalized nutritional guidance tailored to their individual health goals and preferences.

1.1. JUSTIFICATION AND PROBLEM CONTEXT

The global prevalence of diet-related health conditions, including obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular diseases, has reached alarming levels. According to the World Health Organization, unhealthy dietary habits are among the leading risk factors for non-communicable diseases worldwide. Despite the availability of numerous diet tracking applications, a significant gap exists between the technology available and the user experience required for sustained dietary monitoring. This gap represents both a public health challenge and an opportunity for technological innovation.

Traditional diet applications present substantial barriers to consistent usage. Users must navigate through extensive food databases containing thousands of entries, manually search for each food item consumed, and estimate portion sizes using standardized measurements. This process is not only time-consuming but also fails to accommodate the natural way people think and communicate about food. When a user consumes "a bowl of homemade vegetable curry with two rotis," they are forced to decompose this simple meal into multiple database searches, often settling for approximate matches that compromise nutritional accuracy. The cognitive burden of this process leads to user fatigue and ultimately abandonment of dietary tracking habits.

Furthermore, existing systems largely ignore the cultural diversity of dietary practices. Food terminology, preparation methods, and portion conventions vary significantly across cultures and regions. A system designed primarily around Western dietary patterns fails to serve users from diverse backgrounds effectively. This limitation is particularly relevant in multicultural societies and emerging markets where dietary tracking adoption is growing rapidly. The lack of culturally appropriate food entries forces users to make substitutions that undermine the accuracy and relevance of their nutritional data.

The emergence of Large Language Models presents a transformative solution to these challenges. LLMs possess the remarkable ability to understand natural language in all its complexity—interpreting colloquial food descriptions, inferring implicit quantities, recognizing regional food terminology, and extracting structured information from conversational input. By leveraging this capability, we can fundamentally reimagine the dietary tracking experience, making it as simple as describing a meal to a knowledgeable friend. This project harnesses the power of LLMs to bridge the gap between user-friendly interaction and accurate nutritional analysis.

1.2. TOPIC DEFINITION

This project focuses on the development of a Diet LLM Recommendation System—a comprehensive web-based platform that combines natural language processing, nutritional science, and personalized recommendation algorithms to deliver an intelligent dietary guidance solution. The system represents a convergence of multiple technological domains unified by the goal of making nutritional awareness accessible to everyone.

At its core, the system enables users to log meals by simply describing what they ate in natural language. For example, a user might input "I had two eggs with toast and butter for breakfast, along with a glass of orange juice." The LLM processes this input to extract structured meal data, including individual food items, quantities, units, portion sizes, and preparation methods. This structured data is then matched against a curated nutrition database to calculate accurate nutritional values. The entire process occurs seamlessly, transforming a casual meal description into precise nutritional information within seconds.

Unlike generic diet trackers, this system maintains comprehensive user profiles encompassing demographic information, dietary preferences such as vegetarian or low-carb diets, food allergies and intolerances, cultural food preferences, and specific health goals. This rich profile data enables the system to generate highly personalized recommendations that resonate with individual users. The personalization extends beyond simple calorie counting to consider the holistic dietary context of each user.

The system employs LLMs not only for input processing but also for generating personalized daily summaries and dietary recommendations. These recommendations are grounded in the user's actual consumption patterns, progress toward goals, and established nutritional guidelines. Importantly, the system is specifically designed to communicate in a supportive, non-judgmental tone that encourages positive behavioral change rather than inducing guilt or anxiety about food choices.

1.3. AIM OF THE PROJECT

The primary aim of this project is to design, develop, and evaluate an intelligent diet recommendation system that democratizes access to personalized nutritional guidance through the innovative application of Large Language Model technology. This aim encompasses both the technical challenge of building an accurate, responsive system and the broader goal of making dietary awareness more accessible and sustainable for diverse user populations.

The project seeks to develop a robust natural language processing pipeline capable of accurately extracting structured meal information from conversational food descriptions with high precision and recall. This involves careful prompt engineering, handling of ambiguous inputs, and graceful degradation when faced with unusual or unclear descriptions. Alongside this, the project aims to create a comprehensive, culturally inclusive nutrition database that supports diverse food items, regional variations, and multiple preparation methods, ensuring that users from different cultural backgrounds can track their diets accurately.

The implementation of a personalized recommendation engine forms another critical objective. This engine generates actionable, context-aware dietary suggestions based on individual user profiles, goals, and consumption patterns. The recommendations must balance nutritional accuracy with practical applicability, offering suggestions that users can realistically implement in their daily lives. The project also aims to design and build a user-friendly web interface that simplifies the meal logging process and presents nutritional information in an accessible, engaging manner. Finally, the project establishes evaluation methodologies for assessing system accuracy, recommendation quality, and user satisfaction, providing a foundation for continuous improvement.

1.4. SCOPE OF THE WORK

The scope of this project encompasses both the functional capabilities delivered to end users and the technical infrastructure required to support those capabilities. From a functional perspective, the system provides natural language meal logging where users can describe meals in everyday language with the system automatically parsing and structuring the input. The automated nutritional analysis calculates comprehensive nutritional profiles including calories, macronutrients such as protein, carbohydrates, and fat, as well as fiber and sodium content.

User profile management enables complete configuration of user demographics, dietary preferences, allergies, and health goals. The meal history tracking feature provides chronological storage and retrieval of all logged meals with associated nutritional data, allowing users to review their eating patterns over time. Personalized recommendations take the form of AI-generated daily summaries comparing actual intake against goals, accompanied by actionable improvement suggestions. Feedback mechanisms allow users to respond to recommendations, enabling the system to learn and improve its personalization over time.

From a technical perspective, the implementation covers backend development using a RESTful API built with the Python FastAPI framework, SQLAlchemy ORM, and PostgreSQL database. LLM integration connects the system with OpenAI GPT models for natural language understanding and recommendation generation. A secure JWT-based authentication system protects user data and ensures that dietary information remains private. The frontend is developed as a React TypeScript web application with responsive design to support both desktop and mobile access. The database design follows a normalized relational schema supporting users, meals, nutrients, and recommendations with appropriate relationships and indexing.

The project follows an incremental development approach divided into four phases. The current phase delivers a text-based meal logging MVP with core recommendation features. Subsequent phases will introduce Retrieval-Augmented Generation for knowledge-grounded recommendations, image-based meal logging using computer vision, and optimization with scaling and advanced personalization features. Certain capabilities are explicitly excluded from the current scope, including medical diagnosis or clinical dietary prescriptions, real-time integration with wearable devices for activity tracking, meal delivery or food ordering services, and multi-language support beyond English.

1.5. SIGNIFICANT CONTRIBUTIONS

This project makes several significant contributions to the field of intelligent dietary guidance systems. First, it demonstrates a practical, end-to-end application of Large Language Models for dietary tracking—a domain where LLM capabilities have been underexplored. The system showcases how prompt engineering and structured extraction can transform conversational meal descriptions into actionable nutritional data, providing a template for similar applications in health and wellness domains.

Second, unlike existing solutions that predominantly cater to Western dietary patterns, this system is designed from the ground up to support diverse cultural foods. The nutrition database includes regional Indian foods, and the LLM prompts are crafted to recognize culturally specific food terminology and preparation methods. This culturally inclusive design addresses a significant gap in the current landscape of dietary applications.

Third, the recommendation system incorporates psychologically informed communication principles. It is specifically designed to avoid triggering language that could promote unhealthy relationships with food. All communications use positive framing, focus on progress rather than deficits, and avoid judgmental language. This approach addresses growing concerns about the potential negative psychological impacts of diet tracking applications, particularly for users vulnerable to disordered eating.

Fourth, the system architecture is designed for extensibility, with clear separation between the parsing layer, nutrition matching layer, and recommendation layer. This modularity facilitates future enhancements such as RAG-based recommendations and image-based logging without requiring fundamental architectural changes. The design demonstrates how complex AI systems can be structured for maintainability and evolution.

Finally, the project establishes rigorous evaluation methodologies covering parsing accuracy, nutrition matching accuracy, recommendation quality through expert evaluation, and system performance. This comprehensive evaluation framework provides a template for assessing similar AI-powered health applications and contributes to the broader discourse on evaluation standards for LLM-based systems.

CHAPTER 4: PROJECT DESCRIPTION
================================================================================

4.1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

The Diet LLM Recommendation System is an intelligent web-based application that leverages Large Language Models (LLMs) to provide personalized dietary guidance. Users can log their meals using natural language descriptions, and the system automatically extracts structured food data, calculates nutritional values, and generates personalized recommendations based on individual health goals and preferences.

4.2. SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

The system follows a modular, multi-tier architecture comprising four primary layers:

4.2.1. Client Layer
A web-based interface enabling users to:
- Log meals via natural language text descriptions
- View nutritional summaries and breakdowns
- Receive personalized daily recommendations
- Manage profile settings and dietary goals

4.2.2. API Layer
A RESTful backend built on FastAPI, exposing endpoints for:
- User authentication (registration, login, JWT management)
- User profile and goal management
- Meal logging and history retrieval
- Recommendation generation and feedback

4.2.3. AI/ML Layer
Integration of advanced AI capabilities:
- Large Language Models (GPT-4o-mini/GPT-4) for natural language understanding
- Structured extraction of meal data from conversational input
- Recommendation generation with personalized context
- Embedding models for semantic food matching (planned RAG enhancement)

4.2.4. Data Layer
Comprehensive data management:
- PostgreSQL relational database for structured storage
- User profiles, meals, nutrients, and recommendations
- Nutrition database with diverse food entries
- Vector store for semantic search (Phase 2)

4.3. KEY FEATURES

4.3.1. Natural Language Meal Logging
Users describe meals in everyday language: "I had two eggs, toast with butter, and a glass of orange juice for breakfast." The LLM parses this input into structured data including food items, quantities, units, portion sizes, and preparation methods.

4.3.2. Intelligent Food Matching
Parsed food names are matched against a curated nutrition database using:
- Fuzzy string matching for common variations
- Semantic similarity matching for contextual understanding
- Confidence scoring to flag ambiguous items for user confirmation

4.3.3. Automated Nutrition Calculation
The system calculates comprehensive nutritional information:
- Calories
- Macronutrients (protein, carbohydrates, fat)
- Fiber content
- Sodium levels
- Aggregated daily totals

4.3.4. Personalized Recommendations
LLM-generated recommendations consider:
- User profile (age, sex, weight, activity level)
- Dietary preferences and restrictions
- Health goals (calorie targets, macro splits)
- Historical eating patterns (7-day rolling analysis)

4.3.5. User Profile Management
Comprehensive profile settings:
- Demographic information
- Dietary preferences (vegetarian, vegan, low-carb, etc.)
- Allergies and intolerances
- Cultural food preferences
- Daily calorie and macronutrient targets

4.4. DATA FLOW

The system processes meal logging requests through the following pipeline:

1. User Input: Natural language meal description submitted via API
2. LLM Parsing: GPT model extracts structured meal data (JSON format)
3. Food Matching: Parsed food names matched to nutrition database entries
4. Nutrient Calculation: Nutritional values computed based on quantities
5. Database Storage: Meal and nutrient data persisted
6. Recommendation Engine: Daily summaries generated using user context
7. Response: Structured response returned to client

CHAPTER 6: METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

6.1. DEVELOPMENT METHODOLOGY

The project follows an iterative, phased development approach:

| Phase | Focus | Key Deliverables |
|-------|-------|------------------|
| Phase 1 | Text-Only MVP | Backend API, LLM parsing, nutrition calculation, frontend UI |
| Phase 2 | RAG Enhancement | Vector database, knowledge base, semantic matching |
| Phase 3 | Image Logging | Vision model integration, food detection, portion estimation |
| Phase 4 | Optimization | Caching, performance tuning, comprehensive testing |

6.2. LLM-BASED STRUCTURED EXTRACTION

The system uses GPT-4o-mini to convert natural language meal descriptions into structured JSON. The prompt instructs the LLM to extract: food names, quantities, units, portion sizes, preparation methods, and confidence scores (0-1). Low-confidence items are flagged for user confirmation.

Example: "2 eggs with toast" → {"items": [{"food": "eggs", "qty": 2}, {"food": "toast", "qty": 1}]}

6.3. FOOD MATCHING ALGORITHM

| Step | Method | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Fuzzy Search | SQL ILIKE for case-insensitive substring matching |
| 2 | Word Reduction | Try first word only (e.g., "scrambled eggs" → "eggs") |
| 3 | Variations | Try singular/plural forms |
| 4 | Confidence Check | If < 0.7, flag for user confirmation |

6.4. NUTRIENT CALCULATION

For each matched food item:
- factor = quantity / 100 (for grams) OR quantity (for servings)
- nutrients = food.nutrient_per_100g × factor

Aggregate across all items for meal totals (calories, protein, carbs, fat, fiber, sodium).

6.5. DAILY SUMMARY GENERATION

Process: Aggregate daily nutrients → Compare against user goals → Analyze 7-day trends → Generate LLM summary with positive framing and actionable suggestions → Store with metadata.

6.6. RAG FOR RECOMMENDATIONS (Phase 2)

Knowledge base of nutrition documents → Chunk into 200-500 token passages → Generate embeddings → Store in vector database → Retrieve relevant passages for recommendation prompts → Ground LLM output in factual knowledge.

6.7. DATABASE DESIGN

| Table | Purpose | Key Relationships |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| users | Profiles, goals, credentials | Has many meals, recommendations |
| meals | Logged meals with timestamp | Belongs to user, has many items |
| meal_items | Individual foods in meals | References foods table |
| foods | Nutrition database (500+ entries) | Referenced by meal_items |
| nutrient_aggregates | Calculated totals per meal | Belongs to meal |
| recommendations | AI-generated summaries | Belongs to user |

6.8. API DESIGN

| Resource | Endpoints |
|----------|-----------|
| Auth | POST /register, POST /login, GET /me |
| Users | GET /me, PUT /me, PUT /me/goals |
| Meals | POST /log, GET /, GET /{id}, DELETE /{id} |
| Recommendations | GET /daily, POST /generate, POST /{id}/feedback |

All endpoints prefixed with /api/v1/. Protected routes require JWT Bearer token.

7.2. PARSING ACCURACY EVALUATION

7.2.1. Dataset
200 manually annotated meal descriptions with ground-truth structured data covering:
- Simple meals (single items)
- Complex meals (multiple items with varying quantities)
- Ambiguous descriptions (no explicit quantities)
- Regional food terminology

7.2.2. Metrics
- Precision: Ratio of correctly extracted items to total extracted items
- Recall: Ratio of correctly extracted items to total actual items
- F1 Score: Harmonic mean of precision and recall
- Quantity Accuracy: Percentage of quantities within ±10% of ground truth
- Confidence Calibration: Correlation between confidence scores and correctness

7.2.3. Procedure
1. Submit each test meal description to the LLM parsing service
2. Compare extracted JSON output against ground truth annotations
3. Calculate precision, recall, and F1 for food item extraction
4. Evaluate quantity accuracy for matched items
5. Analyze confidence score calibration

7.3. NUTRITION MATCHING EVALUATION

7.3.1. Dataset
500 parsed food names with manually verified matches to nutrition database entries.

7.3.2. Metrics
- Top-1 Accuracy: Percentage of queries where the best match is correct
- Top-3 Accuracy: Percentage of queries where correct match is in top 3
- Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR): Average of reciprocal ranks of correct answers

7.3.3. Procedure
1. Extract food names from parsed meal data
2. Run fuzzy matching algorithm against nutrition database
3. Record top-k candidates for each query
4. Compare against ground truth matches
5. Calculate accuracy metrics

7.4. RECOMMENDATION QUALITY EVALUATION

8.6. DISCUSSION

The results demonstrate that LLM-based meal parsing achieves high accuracy for natural language food logging, significantly reducing the friction of dietary tracking. The 91% F1 score for food item extraction exceeds the usability threshold for practical application.

Nutrition matching shows room for improvement, particularly for regional foods and spelling variations. The planned semantic matching enhancement (Phase 2) is projected to improve Top-1 accuracy by approximately 9 percentage points.

Expert evaluation confirms that LLM-generated recommendations meet quality standards for factual correctness and appropriate tone. The actionability score of 3.9 suggests opportunity for improvement through more specific portion and timing guidance.

System performance is acceptable for the MVP phase, with meal logging completing within the 5-second target. Optimization efforts in Phase 4 will focus on caching and prompt engineering to reduce latency and API costs.
